About Me

Born and raised in western New York, I have lived in New York City for the last 7.5 years and currently reside in the Bedford-Stuyvesant neighborhood of Brooklyn. Outside of researching and teaching philosophy, I enjoy exploring this extraordinary city, listening to music, watching movies, reading about aviation, and spending time with my two cats.

My Approach

My teaching experience has made it evident that students learn differently, and so I adjust my methods to fit clients’ needs. In particular, I emphasize finding a balance in two respects. The first is between providing and withholding answers: this ensures that students get the help they need, but also are on the path to mastering the material for themselves. The second is between offering criticism and support: students will never grow without constructive criticism, but they also need encouragement to build their confidence.
